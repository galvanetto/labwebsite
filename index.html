Welcome! The Galvanetto Lab will open in March 2026 at the Institute of Structural Biology (IBS) in Grenoble. I’m currently setting up the lab and recruiting postdocs, PhD students, and Master’s students. If you’re excited by single-molecule experiments and quantitative biophysics, email me!

What we like to do

We work in experimental biophysics, with a focus on single-molecule experiments. We also venture into computation and theory to better understand what we observe. Above all, we work to maintain the highest possible rigor in everything we do.

Why we do it

We are not fully satisfied with current views on how life works. These views are not necessarily incorrect, but they often feel incomplete. One self-evident example is the prodigious coherence of the molecular processes that occur within a single cell. While these processes can, in principle, be reduced into parts and analyzed individually, they operate as a whole, producing what we broadly call life. It's no surprise that some of the greatest minds of the past sympathized with the unorthodox idea of a "projective force" behind life itself. In the early 21st century, with great technologies in our hands, we believe that exploring these fundamental questions is the most exciting pursuit imaginable. By engaging with these questions, we hope to deepen our understanding of life and offer new insights into disease.
